Introduction  
Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) is a statistical process used to understand datasets through summary 
statistics, visualizations, and relationship analysis before predictive modeling or machine learning.  
This project performs comprehensive EDA on a large -scale industry performance dataset containing 
15,000 records and 12 variables. The analysis focuses on identifying trends across industries, 
company performance metrics, employee distribution, revenue beha vior, and market -related 
patterns.  
 
Situation Overview  
The primary objective of this project is to:  
• Perform univariate, bivariate, and multivariate analysis  
• Detect and interpret outliers  
• Measure correlations between numerical variables  
• Analyze skewness and kurtosis  
• Extract meaningful business insights from industry data  
 
Dataset Overview

• Data Types: Numerical, Categorical, Date

Steps in EDA for Industry Dataset  
Univariate Analysis  
Univariate analysis examines one variable at a time.  
Numerical Variables  
For each numerical column, the following statistical measures were computed:  
• Mean  
• Median  
• Standard Deviation  
• Minimum & Maximum  
• Skewness  
• Kurtosis  
Example Insights  
• annual_revenue_million showed positive skewness indicating that most companies 
generated moderate revenue while few companies generated extremely high revenue.  
• employee_count exhibited high variance due to differences between small and large 
enterprises.  
• profit_margin_percent showed relatively stable distribution across industries.  
• customer_count displayed moderate right skew caused by large customer bases in retail and 
technology sectors.  
 
Categorical Variables

Frequency distributions were analyzed for:  
• industry  
• country  
• region  
Example Findings  
• Technology and Retail industries had the highest number of companies.  
• Majority of companies were located in North America and Asia.  
• Manufacturing companies showed relatively lower market ratings compared to Technology 
companies.  
 
Outlier Detection  
Outliers were detected using:  
• Boxplots  
• Interquartile Range (IQR) Method  
• Z-score Method  
Observations  
• Extreme values were present in annual_revenue_million.  
• High outliers in employee_count represented multinational enterprises.  
• Some companies showed exceptionally high customer_count, especially in the Retail sector.  
• Outliers were retained since they represent genuine large -scale organizations and important 
business scenarios.

Multivariate Analysis  
Multivariate analysis examines interactions among three or more variables.

Skewness and Kurtosis Interpretation  
Skewness  
Positive skewness was observed in:  
• annual_revenue_million  
• customer_count  
• employee_count  
This indicates that most companies operate at medium scale while a smaller number of enterprises 
dominate the market.  
 
Kurtosis  
High kurtosis was observed in:  
• annual_revenue_million  
• employee_count  
This suggests the presence of extreme high -value organizations and large multinational companies.  
Understanding skewness and kurtosis helped identify:  
• Distribution asymmetry  
• Extreme business behaviors  
• Market concentration patterns  
• Revenue dominance by large enterprises
